INTERNATIONAL PRESS CORPS




As a member of the IPC, you will have to represent a national or international news agency instead of a country. In preparation for this role, you must write an editorial instead of a position paper. The reporters will inform the conference about the happenings in the committees through the Press Conferences. It is engaging work, and more importantly it is fun.

ABOUT THE EDITORIALS


US State Abortion Laws:

Before the U.S. Supreme Court's decision (CASE : Roe Vs Wade) decriminalized abortion nationwide in 1973, abortion was already legal in several states. The decision, however, imposed a uniform framework for state legislation on the subject. Several groups have been at play and have vastly influenced the present scenario. Creation of financial and logistical barriers make it difficult or impossible for people to get abortions, despite what the law says. In 2019, several US states passed laws which effectively ban abortion, while others have taken steps to drastically restrict access to abortion. The reporters must send in their editorial pieces at least 24 hours prior to the commencement of the MUN. There is no word limit and reporters are free to use their creativity to express their/their agency’s view.

BRIEF TO THE BEATS



Agenda: Responsible Journalism in the world of Media Bias and Selective ‘Newsing’.

Remaining an Agency Without Bias

There are many different sources of news from the international press. Although reporters cover issues that are relevant to the nation and audience that views their media, it is not justified to use significant bias in their writing. What this means is that although a reporter is not obligated to cover certain stories, if they do cover a story, they should do so with total integrity and free from bias.

The committee shall discuss and aim to define a standard code to prevent any kind of bias in their reporting and preserve the integrity of the press.

Dear Delegates,

Welcome to RMUN 2019!! We are extremely honored and excited to serve as the council for the International Press Corps.

The press has the privilege and responsibility of keeping the public informed and keeping those in power accountable! Over the course of the two days, you will assume the role of an international news agency and cover the events of the conference through that perspective.

Each news organization is unique in its tone, audience, slant, and editorial stance. As a journalist, you will be breaking stories, holding delegates accountable to their positions, communicating breakthroughs to the entire conference, along with exhibiting your flair in writing and eye for minute details. You will get to interview delegates, write news articles, release press briefs and opinion pieces.

The role of the Press Corps at RMUN 2019 and in the workings of international democracy cannot be understated. We hope that the first-hand experiences you will get as a journalist will further instill in you the importance of a free and independent press.

We are here to assist you while you prepare for the committee and beyond. Please reach out to us with any questions or concerns you might have - even if it’s just getting to know each other better!

We look forward to meeting you all and hope you have fun at this year’s conference!



Prajval Sairam Krishnan (Head of Press)
